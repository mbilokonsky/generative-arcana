# Traditional Tarot Structure Reference

This document provides the canonical structure of traditional tarot to serve as a reference point when designing custom decks.

## Traditional Dialectics

The traditional Rider-Waite-Smith tarot is built on two primary dialectics:

1. **Active vs Passive** (or Dynamic vs Receptive)
2. **Masculine vs Feminine** (or Yang vs Yin)

These create a two-dimensional semantic space that can be divided into four quadrants, which become the four suits.

## Traditional Suits

The four suits emerge from the cross-product of the dialectics:

| Suit | Dialectic Combination | Element | Core Meanings |
|------|----------------------|---------|---------------|
| **Swords** | Active + Masculine | Air | Intellect, conflict, truth, clarity, decisiveness, communication |
| **Wands** | Passive + Masculine | Fire | Creativity, passion, inspiration, energy, will, growth |
| **Pentacles** | Active + Feminine | Earth | Material world, resources, practical matters, body, manifestation |
| **Cups** | Passive + Feminine | Water | Emotions, relationships, intuition, feelings, love, connection |

## The Fool's Journey (Major Arcana)

The 22 Major Arcana cards tell the story of the Fool's spiritual journey through life:

0. **The Fool** - Innocence, new beginnings, spontaneity
1. **The Magician** - Manifestation, resourcefulness, power
2. **The High Priestess** - Intuition, sacred knowledge, subconscious
3. **The Empress** - Femininity, beauty, nature, abundance
4. **The Emperor** - Authority, structure, control, father figure
5. **The Hierophant** - Tradition, conformity, spiritual wisdom
6. **The Lovers** - Love, harmony, relationships, values alignment
7. **The Chariot** - Control, willpower, success, action
8. **Strength** - Courage, inner strength, compassion, influence
9. **The Hermit** - Soul searching, introspection, inner guidance
10. **Wheel of Fortune** - Good luck, karma, life cycles, destiny
11. **Justice** - Justice, fairness, truth, cause and effect
12. **The Hanged Man** - Pause, surrender, letting go, new perspective
13. **Death** - Endings, change, transformation, transition
14. **Temperance** - Balance, moderation, patience, purpose
15. **The Devil** - Shadow self, attachment, addiction, restriction
16. **The Tower** - Sudden change, upheaval, chaos, revelation
17. **The Star** - Hope, faith, purpose, renewal, spirituality
18. **The Moon** - Illusion, fear, anxiety, subconscious, intuition
19. **The Sun** - Positivity, fun, warmth, success, vitality
20. **Judgement** - Reflection, reckoning, inner calling, absolution
21. **The World** - Completion, accomplishment, travel, fulfillment

### Story Arc Structure

The Fool's Journey can be divided into three acts:

- **Act I (0-7)**: Awareness - The Fool encounters external archetypes and learns about the world
- **Act II (8-14)**: Integration - The Fool faces internal challenges and develops wisdom
- **Act III (15-21)**: Transcendence - The Fool confronts shadow, achieves transformation, reaches completion

## Abstract Numbered Ranks (Minor Arcana)

The numbered cards (Ace through 10) represent an abstract narrative framework that exists independently of but in dialogue with the Major Arcana journey:

| Rank | Abstract Theme | Core Meaning |
|------|---------------|--------------|
| **Ace** | Pure potential, initiation | The seed or spark; raw, unformed energy of the suit |
| **Two** | Duality, choice, partnership | Balance, decision, tension between two forces |
| **Three** | Growth, creation, expansion | Initial manifestation; creative tension produces result |
| **Four** | Stability, foundation, structure | Consolidation; the energy finds form and stability |
| **Five** | Conflict, challenge, instability | Disruption; testing of the established structure |
| **Six** | Harmony, communication, flow | Resolution; restoration of balance at a higher level |
| **Seven** | Assessment, reflection, crossroads | Taking stock; choosing direction for continued growth |
| **Eight** | Movement, change, mastery | Active transformation; applying learned skills |
| **Nine** | Fulfillment, integration, near-completion | Maturity; the energy of the suit reaches its peak |
| **Ten** | Completion, overflow, transition | Culmination; end of cycle prepares for new beginning |

### Relationship to Major Arcana

While the Major Arcana tells a linear story of spiritual development, the numbered ranks represent a cyclical pattern that can be applied to any domain. They are not the "first ten cards" of the Major Arcana but rather an abstracted, repeating pattern of development that plays out differently in each suit.

## Face Cards (Court Cards)

The traditional face cards represent archetypal characters or personas:

| Rank | Traditional Role | Typical Characteristics |
|------|------------------|------------------------|
| **Page** | Student, messenger | Youthful energy, curiosity, beginning to learn the suit's lessons |
| **Knight** | Warrior, quester | Active pursuit, extreme expression, the suit's energy in motion |
| **Queen** | Nurturer, ruler | Mature feminine expression, internalized mastery, receptive power |
| **King** | Authority, master | Mature masculine expression, externalized mastery, active power |

### Face Card Progression

The traditional progression follows a developmental/hierarchical model:
- Page → Knight represents moving from learning to action
- Knight → Queen represents moving from external action to internal mastery
- Queen → King represents moving from receptive power to directive power

However, this is not the only possible structure. Face cards can follow other patterns:
- **Cyclical**: Spring → Summer → Autumn → Winter
- **Alchemical**: Nigredo → Albedo → Citrinitas → Rubedo
- **Compositional**: Element → Compound → Mixture → Synthesis
- **Perspectival**: Self → Other → Community → World

## Prime/Composite Structure of the Major Arcana

The 22 Major Arcana cards (numbered 0-21) have an underlying mathematical structure that can be used to generate coherent, deeply interconnected archetypes.

### The Identities

- **0** — The additive identity. Adding zero changes nothing. This is the void, the precondition, pure potential before differentiation. The Fool.
- **1** — The multiplicative identity. Multiplying by one changes nothing. This is the unit of agency, the transparent operator that enables all further action without distorting it. The Magician.

### The Primes

Eight cards occupy prime-numbered positions: **2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19**

Primes are numbers divisible only by 1 and themselves. In tarot terms, these represent *irreducible* archetypal energies — experiences that cannot be factored into simpler components. They simply *are*.

| Position | Traditional Card | Prime Properties |
|----------|-----------------|------------------|
| 2 | High Priestess | First prime. Appears in factors of 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18, 20 |
| 3 | Empress | Second prime. Appears in factors of 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21 |
| 5 | Hierophant | Third prime. Appears in factors of 10, 15, 20 |
| 7 | Chariot | Fourth prime. Appears in factors of 14, 21 |
| 11 | Justice/Strength | Fifth prime. First prime in Act II |
| 13 | Death | Sixth prime. Irreducible transformation |
| 17 | Star | Seventh prime. First prime in Act III |
| 19 | Sun | Eighth prime. Final prime before completion |

**Note:** Cards 2 and 3 are particularly load-bearing — they appear as factors in most composite cards. When designing custom decks, these two primes must be chosen carefully as energies that combine well and scale meaningfully when raised to powers.

### The Composites

Eleven cards occupy composite-numbered positions: **4, 6, 8, 9, 10, 12, 14, 15, 16, 18, 20, 21**

Composites are products of primes. In tarot terms, these represent *derived* archetypal energies — experiences that emerge from the combination of irreducible elements.

#### Interpreting Powers

When a prime is raised to a power, its energy intensifies or transforms:

- **x²** (squared) — The energy stabilizes, structures, or concentrates. What was dynamic becomes a framework. In traditional tarot: Emperor (2²) as structure/authority built from the Priestess's hidden knowledge.
- **x³** (cubed) — Deep mastery or its shadow: alienation, excess, being consumed by. The energy has compounded past comfortable limits.
- **x⁴** (fourth power) — Too much. Collapse, breakthrough, shattering. The structure built by x² has been squared again and cannot hold. In traditional tarot: The Tower (2⁴) as the Priestess's energy concentrated past the point of containment.

#### Interpreting Products

When two different primes multiply, their energies combine:

- **x × y** — Ask: "What happens when energy X meets energy Y?" The combination may be harmonious, tensional, or transformative depending on the primes.
- **x² × y** — The first prime is structured/stabilized; the second modifies or acts upon that structure. 
- **x × y²** — The second prime is fulfilled/complete; the first prime witnesses or interacts with that fulfillment.

#### Composite Reference Table

| Position | Traditional Card | Factorization | Interpretive Frame |
|----------|-----------------|---------------|---------------------|
| 4 | Emperor | 2² | High Priestess squared. Hidden knowledge stabilized into visible structure. The Priestess holds secrets behind the veil; the Emperor has built a framework *from* those secrets. Intuition becomes law. The threshold becomes a throne. |

### Distribution Across the Journey

The prime/composite distribution creates natural structure in the three-act journey:

- **Act I (0-7)**: Four primes (2, 3, 5, 7) — prime-dense. The protagonist encounters fundamental, irreducible experiences.
- **Act II (8-14)**: Two primes (11, 13) — composite-heavy. Earlier energies combine; complexity emerges.
- **Act III (15-21)**: Two primes (17, 19) — composite-heavy. Culmination through combination, with late primes offering transcendent irreducibility.

### Using This Structure

When designing custom Major Arcana:

1. Define the identities (0, 1) first — these are the preconditions
2. Define the eight primes as irreducible archetypal energies within the theme
3. Derive the composites by meaningfully combining their prime factors
4. Verify the sequence forms a coherent narrative arc

The mathematical structure is generative scaffolding. If composites don't make sense as combinations of their factors, revisit the prime definitions. The goal is cards that feel *inevitably derived* from the atomic energies that precede them.

## Prime Structure in the Minor Arcana (Optional)

The prime/composite framework can also be applied to the numbered Minor Arcana ranks (Ace-10), though more lightly than in the Majors. Here the suits are doing most of the structural work; the ranks provide a secondary axis.

### Structure

- **Ace (1)** — Multiplicative identity. Pure suit potential.
- **2** — First prime. An irreducible question.
- **3** — Second prime. An irreducible question.
- **4 = 2²** — The 2-question stabilized.
- **5** — Third prime. An irreducible question.
- **6 = 2×3** — The 2-question meets the 3-question.
- **7** — Fourth prime. An irreducible question.
- **8 = 2³** — The 2-question cubed — mastery or excess.
- **9 = 3²** — The 3-question fulfilled.
- **10 = 2×5** — The 2-question meets the 5-question. Cycle completes.

### Traditional Mapping

If we read the traditional RWS numbered ranks through this lens:

| Rank | Structure | Traditional Meaning | Prime Reading |
|------|-----------|--------------------| --------------|
| Ace | Identity | Pure potential | The unit of suit energy |
| 2 | Prime | Duality, choice, balance | Irreducible: the first split |
| 3 | Prime | Growth, creation, expansion | Irreducible: the first synthesis |
| 4 | 2² | Stability, foundation | Duality stabilized into structure |
| 5 | Prime | Conflict, challenge, instability | Irreducible: disruption that cannot be factored |
| 6 | 2×3 | Harmony, communication, flow | Duality × Growth = resolution, integration |
| 7 | Prime | Assessment, reflection, crossroads | Irreducible: the question that stops you |
| 8 | 2³ | Movement, change, mastery | Duality cubed — you've lived the split so long it becomes power |
| 9 | 3² | Fulfillment, near-completion | Growth fulfilled — synthesis synthesized |
| 10 | 2×5 | Completion, overflow, transition | Duality × Disruption = the cycle meets its own destabilization |

### Usage Note

This is optional scaffolding. The Minor Arcana can be designed with other frameworks (narrative progression, elemental associations, etc.). Use the prime structure if it helps generate coherent rank meanings, but don't force it if another approach fits the theme better.

## The Chaldean Decan Order — the traditional transversal

Traditional tarot lays a third structure across the suit×rank grid: an astrological axis organized by the **Chaldean order** of the seven classical planets. Generative Arcana's transversal axis generalizes this. The original clarifies what the generalization preserves.

### The Chaldean order

The seven classical planets, by apparent speed (slowest to fastest):

**Saturn → Jupiter → Mars → Sun → Venus → Mercury → Moon**

Canonical, not arbitrary — it also generates the order of the days of the week (via planetary hours). The sequence *means* something: a descent from the slow, heavy, outer bounds inward to the swift, personal Moon.

### How it cross-cuts the deck

Traditionally the planets reach the cards via the **36 decans** — the zodiac's 360° in 36 ten-degree arcs, each with a planetary ruler cycling in Chaldean order. The decans map to the 36 pip cards (2–10 across 4 suits). Because **7 does not divide 36** (36 = 5×7 + 1), the cycle never realigns with the suit grid: a planet's "family" is a diagonal the suits cannot see. The trumps additionally carry direct planetary attributions (seven of the majors *are* a planet).

### What Generative Arcana keeps, and what it changes

The transversal abstracts the *function*, not the planets:

1. **A small fixed alphabet of N stations** (here, 7).
2. **A canonical order that carries meaning** (here, slowest→fastest).
3. **N skew to the grid**, so the cycle refuses to align and produces cross-cutting families.
4. **A per-card station** — a coordinate that pins down cards the suit×rank intersection leaves under-determined.
5. **Sublimation by default** — felt, not depicted, except where a card's own needs surface it.

Two deliberate departures from the historical system:

- **It covers every card, not just the 36 pips.** Aces, courts, and all 22 majors carry a station. Tradition gets its cross-cutting for free: it walks only 36 decans (coprime to 7) and maps each suit to a *triplicity* — three signs 120° apart — so a suit samples the continuous planetary walk at scattered, non-adjacent positions. We keep all 56 minors under the walk and use atomic suits (no signs inside them), so we supply that de-alignment explicitly: the minor walk is `station = (rank + k·suit) mod N` with a per-suit kick `k` coprime to N (origin at the first suit's Ace), and the majors are a separate `number mod N` walk from Major 0. (See `references/schema.md` → "The walk.") The diagonal (`k = 1`) is not the *letter* of the decan system but a faithful reduction of its cross-cutting *behavior* to our simpler grid.
- **No stratified visibility or scope machinery.** One transversal, one walk per arcana, uniform sublimation; surfacing is a per-card judgment, not a class rule.

### What a decan captures — and how to generalize it

A decan is a *third of a sign*, and its planet is not a second category bolted on but a **modulation**: the sign (→ suit/element) says what the card broadly *is*; the planet colors which *face* it shows — its temperament, mode, the key it is played in. (The decans were literally called "faces.") Structurally a decan is the cell where two independent symbol systems intersect — a 12-fold zodiac and a 7-fold planetary register — and because those counts don't align, almost every (sign, planet) pairing is nearly unique and recurs only rarely. The planet never changes *what* the card is; it changes *how it sounds*.

Lifted off astrology, a transversal is **a small, cyclically-recurring, canonically-ordered register of *qualities* — orthogonal to the suit×rank grid — that modulates the temperament/mode/face of each card without recategorizing it.** Five properties make one real rather than decorative:

1. **Qualitative, not categorical.** Suit and rank sort cards into buckets (*what* they are). The transversal sets a *quality* (in what key, what mood, what mode). Planets are temperaments, not things.
2. **It recurs.** A small alphabet whose members return periodically, so each quality accretes a family across the deck.
3. **Orthogonal to the grid.** It tracks neither suit nor rank; the moiré between its period and the grid is what makes each full coordinate feel singular.
4. **A meaningful canonical order.** Chaldean order is the speed gradient — outer/slow/structural → inner/fast/personal. A good transversal's order *is* a gradient or circuit, never an arbitrary list.
5. **It modulates without naming itself** — sublimated; the weather, not the subject.

Finding the right transversal for a theme is finding the theme's *second worldview* — a complete, orthogonal register of qualities carrying its own internal order — and letting it interfere with the theme's category system. That interference is most of the deck's richness. (The prime/composite axis, `references/numeric_axis.md`, is then a third interfering register — arithmetic, aperiodic — beating against the periodic transversal.)

### The fourth axis

The **prime/composite structure of the trumps** (documented above) is not a major-only curiosity — it is a function of any card's number, present on the minors (1–14) too. Generative Arcana lifts it out as the fourth, intrinsic axis (`references/numeric_axis.md`), and notices that it *beats against* the transversal: the transversal cycles periodically while the primes recur arithmetically, and where both say "irreducible" a card is doubly-atomic.
